Movement and Migration


Source: Circumpolar Action Plan
    Data from satellite telemetry transmitters on female polar bears have shown that they do not wander aimlessly, but that their movements and distribution are determined by the way they use the sea ice habitat as a platform for feeding, mating, denning and, in some subpopulations, summer retreat areas.
    They tend to move on drifting ice to remain in productive habitats (e.g., over the continental shelf where seals are abundant), which often means moving against the direction of drift of the sea ice to remain in the same general geographic location.
